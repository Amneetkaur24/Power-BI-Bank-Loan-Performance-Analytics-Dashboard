# Loan Portfolio Insights

## Portfolio Health Overview

The analysis indicates that the loan portfolio maintains a strong repayment profile. Approximately 86% of issued loans are classified as good loans, while 13.8% fall under the bad loan category. This distribution suggests a relatively stable loan portfolio with manageable credit risk exposure.

## Lending Volume Trends

Loan funding shows consistent growth across the year, reaching peak funding levels in the final months. This pattern may reflect increased lending activity toward the end of the financial period.

Monitoring these trends helps financial institutions anticipate capital requirements and manage lending capacity.

## Loan Term Distribution

Long-term loans (60 months) represent the majority of funded loans compared to short-term loans (36 months). This indicates a portfolio structure leaning toward extended repayment periods.

While longer loan terms may increase total interest income, they also introduce higher long-term credit risk exposure.

## Borrower Characteristics

Borrowers with longer employment history (10+ years) account for the largest share of funded loans. This suggests that lending decisions may favor financially stable applicants with consistent employment records.

Home ownership analysis shows that both mortgage holders and renters represent significant portions of the loan portfolio, indicating diverse borrower segments.

## Loan Status Performance

A large portion of the funded loan amount is associated with fully paid loans, indicating strong repayment behavior across the portfolio.

However, a smaller proportion of loans classified as charged-off represents potential credit losses that require monitoring.

## Strategic Implications

Based on the analysis, lending teams may consider:

• Strengthening credit risk monitoring for long-term loans
• Expanding lending toward stable employment segments
• Evaluating borrower risk factors in regions with higher loan volumes
• Monitoring charged-off loans to improve credit assessment models

Overall, the dashboard provides a comprehensive view of lending activity and supports better credit risk management decisions.
